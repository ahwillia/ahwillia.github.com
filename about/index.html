About Me

I am currently a PhD student in Neuroscience at UC San Diego, funded through the DOE Computational Science Graduate Fellowship Program. Previously, I was a research technician in Eve Marder's Lab at Brandeis University, where I worked on models of homeostatic plasticity in neurons, the effects of temperature on neural oscillators, and inter-animal variability in the effects of neuromodulators on invertebrate circuits. I recieved my Bachelor's degree from Bowdoin College; I completed a senior thesis in Patsy Dickinson's lab on a computational model of the cardiac CPG in lobsters.

In my spare time I like to run, hike, and play pickup basketball and soccer. I am also writing a open-access online textbook on computational neuroscience (www.Neuro101.com). The first few chapters have been completed and will be uploaded soon.
