About Me

I became seriously interested in neuroscience when I was in high school. Its a condition that probably traces back to my parents - both of whom are psychologists. So, like any normal kid, I grew up reading a lot of Steven Pinker, Noam Chomsky, and Psych 101 textbooks. I attended Bowdoin College from 2008-2012. I was initially interested in pursuing a Psych major, but ended up falling in love with computational modeling.

Currently, I work as a research technician in Eve Marder's lab at Brandeis University. Eve and Patsy (my undergrad advisor at Bowdoin) both work on small invertebrate networks involved in simple, rhythmic motor behaviors (e.g. chewing). My work mostly examines how neurons maintain stable activity patterns over long time periods, when the proteins (e.g. ion channels) that determine neuronal activity are produced and degraded on much quicker time scales.

In my spare time I like to run, hike, and shoot hoops. If I ever moved to California, I would be interested in learning how to surf.
